Tips to Lower your Personal Carbon Emissions

Carbon Score Back to Home
  • 1. Switch to a reusable water bottle instead of plastic ones. It's not only cost-effective but also better for the environment.
  • 2. Try walking or cycling for short distances. It's a healthy addition to your routine, and a mile can be covered in under 20 minutes.
  • 3. Remember to turn off lights and unplug electronics when not in use. Small steps make a big difference.
  • 4. Maintain proper tire pressure and regular servicing for your car. It improves efficiency, reduces fuel consumption,
    and lowers emissions.
  • 5. Opt for locally sourced foods and reduce red meat consumption. Enjoy the fresh taste, support local businesses, and feel healthier.
  • 6. Use the cold wash setting for laundry and always wash full loads. This saves water and energy, and consider air-drying
    clothes for extra savings.
  • 7. Adjust your thermostat to 78°F in summer and 67°F in winter. Turn off heating and cooling when away to significantly cut
    down on energy bills.
  • 8. Drive smarter. Gentle acceleration, coasting to stops, obeying speed limits, and avoiding idle time in drive-thrus can reduce
    fuel use and emissions.
  • 9. Prevent waste by selling, donating, or recycling items you don't need. Aim to reuse or repurpose as much as possible.
  • 10. Choose public transportation, carpooling, or biking for commuting at least once a week. It's a stress-free way to travel
    and good for the planet.
